# DupeTroop — User Journeys
**Date:** April 14, 2026

---

## Overview

Four primary user archetypes describe how people find and use DupeTroop. Most users fall into more than one of these over time.

---

## Journey 1: The Discoverer

**Entry point:** Search engine (e.g. "Cirque Colors dupe")

The most common path. The user has a specific polish in mind and wants to know if a cheaper or more accessible alternative exists. They land directly on a polish detail page or dupe comparison page from search results, read community accuracy scores and opinions, and may sign up to add their own rating.

**Key moments:**
- Lands on `/polishes/[brand]/[slug]` or `/dupes/[id]` from Google
- Reads color/finish/formula accuracy scores
- Decides to sign up and leave an opinion

---

## Journey 2: The Researcher

**Entry point:** Home page or direct navigation

The user is considering buying an expensive indie polish and wants to know if it's worth it versus a drugstore dupe. They search for the polish by name, find its dupe page, read the community's detailed accuracy breakdown, and make a buy-or-skip decision.

**Key moments:**
- Uses search (Cmd+K or hero bar) to find a specific polish
- Compares accuracy scores across multiple dupes
- Uses the information to make a purchase decision

---

## Journey 3: The Browser

**Entry point:** Home page

A casual visitor who isn't looking for anything specific. They scroll through featured and recently-added dupes, discover polishes they didn't know about, and may click into a finish or color category that catches their eye.

**Key moments:**
- Browses the hero, featured dupes, and "Browse by finish" section
- Clicks a finish chip (e.g. Holo) to explore a category
- Discovers a new brand through a dupe card

---

## Journey 4: The Contributor

**Entry point:** Authenticated, returns to the site

An engaged community member who already owns both a polish and its dupe IRL. They submit the pair through the 3-step wizard, rate dupes they've personally compared (color/finish/formula with notes), and vote on whether other community opinions were helpful.

**Key moments:**
- Logs in and navigates to Submit a Dupe
- Completes the 3-step submission wizard
- Returns later to rate dupes and vote on opinions

---

## Gaps Identified

Areas that don't have clear solutions yet, surfaced during this discussion:

- **No ownership tracking** — no way to mark polishes you own or want
- **No contributor feedback loop** — users don't know when their submitted dupe gets approved
- **Home page curation is static** — featured and recent dupes are the only editorial surfaces
- **Search is name-only** — no way to describe what you're looking for (e.g. "cream red under $15")
- **No visual color discovery** — browsing by color family exists but no color-picker-style interface
